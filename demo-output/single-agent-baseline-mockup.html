Northstar Air · Canceled-flight recovery

A recovery flow that tells you what happened, what you're owed, and what's next.

A redesign of the overnight cancellation experience: one honest SMS plus four mobile screens that lead with cause and entitlements, default to rebooking, and end on a usable confirmation — not a "Done" button.

01

The text that wakes you up at 10:46 PM

The current SMS hides the event behind "schedule irregularity." The redesigned version names the cancellation, names the cause, signals that support exists, and offers a path to a human in the same message.

Your 6:15 AM flight NS482 (DEN → LGA) was canceled due to a crew shortage. You have options — including a hotel tonight if you need one. Tap to rebook or get help. Reply HELP for a live agent.

What the SMS does

  • Names the event in plain English. "Canceled" instead of "schedule irregularity."
  • Names the cause. A reason is easier to act on than a euphemism.
  • Signals entitlements early. The phrase "including a hotel tonight" defuses the calculation that drives the call to support.
  • Two paths from one message. Self-serve (tap) or human (reply HELP) — neither is buried.

03

The rules this flow obeys

i.

Cause before choice

Every screen leads with what happened or what's owed before asking the traveler to pick anything.

ii.

No selling during recovery

Fare differences disappear. Standby is explained. "Recommended" is replaced with a specific reason.

iii.

Entitlements are defaults

Hotel and meal credit are pre-selected when owed — the traveler opts out, not in.

iv.

A human is always one tap away

"Talk to agent" appears in the header of every in-app screen and in the entry SMS.

Mockup uses placeholder seat, gate, hotel, and meal-credit values. Actual amounts and qualification rules come from Northstar policy. Per the brief, no metrics, user quotes, or compensation rules are invented. See demo-output/single-agent-baseline.md for the full recommendation, accessibility/trust risks, and experiment plan.
