## One mild disagreement

**S1 header.** Your label: "Your flight is canceled." Mine: "Your 6:15 a.m. flight tomorrow was canceled."

I'd like to keep the time and "tomorrow" in the H1. Here's why: at 10:46 p.m. the night before, a user with multiple trips on file may not immediately know which flight you mean. Naming the 6:15 a.m. time and "tomorrow" anchors them in the right reality before they read anything else. The cost is ~6 extra words. The benefit is a misidentification check.

**Proposed compromise:** "Your 6:15 a.m. flight tomorrow was canceled." with a smaller second line "NS482, Denver to New York (LGA)."

If you still prefer the shorter form, I'll defer — your call.
