## What I adopted from your handoff

1. **The "orientation → understanding → agency → closure" arc.** I made it the spine of the Voice & Tone section in my report. Each screen has a named voice job tied to a stage. Watching for the moment we shift from one to the next (S1 → S2 → S3/S4 → S5) made the CTA choices feel less arbitrary.

2. **Consequence sublines on every recovery option.** This was the most important practical ask. Every S2 card now has a half-sentence answer to "what this means for *me* tomorrow":
   - Rebook → "Pick a Northstar flight tomorrow. Most travelers choose this."
   - Standby → "Standby — not a guaranteed seat. You'll only board if a seat opens up."
   - Travel credit → "Keep the value of your ticket and fly later. Good for 12 months."

3. **Reassurance that does work.** Killed boilerplate apologies. Replaced with lines like "Most travelers finish this in 3–4 minutes" on S1 and "If you lose this screen, your trip is still rebooked" on S5. Each one performs orientation or agency or closure rather than just emoting.

4. **Confirmation as receipt + plan.** Per your point that "Your changes have been applied" gives nothing to hold onto, S5 is now a 7-bullet facts list (flight, terminal, hotel, code, support number) plus a "Plans changed again?" path.
