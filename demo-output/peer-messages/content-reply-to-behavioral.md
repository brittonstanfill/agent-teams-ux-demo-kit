## What I adopted

### 1. Loss-aversion: only when factually true
Aligned. Nowhere in the draft do I use "don't miss / don't lose" framing. When we surface hotel/meal help, we lead with the offer ("you may qualify — we'll check"), not the threat of loss.

### 2. "Recommended" carries its reason
Already integrated from a11y and IA. Final shape: **"Recommended: earliest arrival, no extra cost"** — one line, named reason, no naked authority label.

### 3. No invented urgency
Adopted. No countdown timers on the recovery options screen. The brief's "Most travelers finish this in 3–4 minutes" microcopy on S1 is descriptive, not pressure — flagging it for your pressure-test if you have cycles.

### 4. Fare-difference framing — this was a real catch
**Adopted in full.** Updated S3 flight cards:
- Was: "$84 fare difference" / "$0"
- Now: **"+$84 vs. your original ticket"** / **"Same fare as your original ticket"**
- Suppressed the prominent "$0" chip per your guidance — absence of price chip *is* the message.

This reframes the anchor away from the cheapest recovery option and toward what the user already paid for. Good behavioral catch; I had not been thinking about it that way.

### 5. SMS opener
Aligned. New SMS leads with the event in plain language ("Your 6:15 a.m. Northstar flight NS482 (DEN→LGA) tomorrow was canceled. Tap for options including hotel and meal help: [link]"). Anxiety + ambiguity → concrete next action in one tap.

### 6. Confirmation = peak-end
Aligned. S5 is now a 7-bullet facts list (flight, terminal, gate note, arrival, hotel status, confirmation code, support number) with "save this screen" and "plans changed again?" affordances. No marketing. No "Done."

### 7. Decline copy — no confirmshaming
**Adopted as a missing pattern.** Added neutral, equal-weight decline labels:
- Hotel offer: Primary "Check what I qualify for" / Secondary "Skip for now" (NOT "No thanks, I'll figure it out myself")
- Recovery-option dismissals: name the action neutrally ("Pick a different option," not "I'll figure it out")
- I'll audit the rest of the flow before lock for any guilt-laden decline labels and rewrite them.

## Your open question — phrase library for hedged eligibility

You're right that vague phrasing can re-trigger trust loss. Here's the library, also added to the role report:

| Phrase | Use when |
|---|---|
| **"You qualify for X."** | Check has returned yes. |
| **"X is available for this cancellation."** | Policy-guaranteed for this cancellation type. |
| **"You may qualify for X. We'll check now."** | Genuinely unknown; we're about to check; answer in ≤10 seconds. |
| **"X isn't offered for this cancellation type."** | Check returned no. |
| **"If you book X yourself, save the receipt — we'll review."** | No coverage, but review path exists. |

**The rule that makes the hedge ethical:** never use "you may qualify" as a teaser. Every hedge must resolve to a definite answer in the same flow, on the same screen, within ~10 seconds. A hedge that doesn't resolve is exactly the trust-loss pattern you flagged.
