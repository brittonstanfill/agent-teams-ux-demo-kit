What this flow refuses to do

  • recommendationUse the word “irregularity.” The SMS names the event in plain language.
  • recommendationDefault to travel credit when the user is mid-trip. The orient screen lays paths flat and lets the traveler pick.
  • recommendationBury hotel and meal support in a collapsed FAQ. They are surfaced on the orient screen and given a screen of their own.
  • recommendationHide refund eligibility behind a support call when the airline canceled the flight.
  • recommendationPromise compensation, wait times, or hotel amounts in copy. Anything operational is dynamic and labeled as such.

Accessibility & trust posture

  • recommendationTouch targets at or above 44 px; focus ring uses a high-contrast warm hue that survives both themes.
  • recommendationColor is never the only signal — every status uses an icon or word as well.
  • recommendationLive status changes (e.g., confirmation, callback availability) announced via aria-live.
  • recommendationConfirmation is saved offline and mirrored to email and a wallet pass, so a low-bandwidth traveler isn’t stranded by their phone.
  • recommendation“Talk to a person” is present on every authenticated screen, not gated behind the recovery decision.
