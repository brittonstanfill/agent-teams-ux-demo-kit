Cancelled at 10:46 p.m. — and still home by tomorrow night.

A recovery experience for the moment a flight gets canceled late at night. The job: tell people what happened, show every choice (including a person), and put the care they're owed in front of them — not behind a FAQ link.

01 / Alert

Tell the truth, fast.

The current SMS hides the event behind "schedule irregularity." We name what happened, when, and the single next step — and we never bury the option to talk to a person. [recommendation]

02 / Choose

Three honest paths. No default.

The current flow defaults to "Travel credit," which is rarely the user's goal. We show all three paths flat, in plain consequences, and surface "talk to a person" as a peer — not a fallback. [recommendation]

03 / Choose flight

Sortable, filterable, family-aware.

The current cards lean on an unexplained "Recommended" badge and surface fare differences during a disruption. 04 / Confirm + care

Confirm in the same place you claim care.

Hotel and meal entitlements used to live inside a collapsed "Other policies" FAQ. We move them into the confirm step so the user can claim them in the same breath they confirm — and so they can see they're not being asked to fight for them. [recommendation]
