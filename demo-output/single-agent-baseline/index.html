Canceled flight recovery — mobile

It's 10:46 p.m. Your flight just got canceled. What should the app do next?

Five mobile screens for a tired traveler trying to get home. Trust, accessibility, and behavioral guardrails are annotated under each screen.

Reversibility is named. "Nothing is locked" — directly counters the abandonment risk of a flow that feels final.

Offline-by-default for the receipt. Low-bandwidth users can still show their plan at the desk.

Confirmation announced via aria-live="polite", so a screen reader user knows the save happened without re-scanning the screen.

What this prototype does not invent

  • No specific compensation amount, voucher value, hotel partner, or phone wait time is asserted as fact. Numbers shown on each screen are illustrative of how the system would render real values.
  • Hotel and meal eligibility appears as a check the system performs, not a guaranteed promise. If the actual operations policy is narrower than what the screens suggest, the eligibility logic — not the copy — is what changes.
  • Fare-difference language ("None for this rebooking") is a recommendation for cancellation flows, not a claim about today's pricing rules. See the recommendation memo for the operations dependency.
  • "Most people pick this" on the Choose-a-path screen would be backed by real, current behavioral data or removed. It is annotated here as a behavioral guardrail, not a manipulator.
